"Love is the one thing that actually completes the picture"

If you have scrolled this far and finally ended up on my profile, then I value you a lot as a reader. Nothing but a hello to you, and I am glad you somehow found me. I am a very simple guy. During the day, I work a 9-to-5 job with a clothing company as an operations associate (technically, if you have a garment issue, I am probably the best person to talk to). By evening, I like driving around with no place to go and wandering near the side of a small stream; other than that, I enjoy gardening, playing online games with friends, and I am also an active moderator for the YatrAmore.

Let us rewind to how I met Yaza in school when I was 16. This was back in the Stone Age when we had no text messages or phones to call; we actually had to see each other in person to hang out. Minimal technology makes deeper bonds. We did everything together, like playing in school, creating chaos in class, and travelling on crowded buses. I am 32 now (2026), and I have spent half my life knowing him. He transitioned from a school friend to a brother somewhere along the way.

In between, he packed his bag and said goodbye so he could move to Italy for higher studies. Everything then continued over text messages and calls. One day, he called and introduced me to his best friend. I spoke to her, and I added her. It was fun talking to her over the years. The best words were always “This is good” in most of our conversations. She is actually one of the most caring people on the planet, the first girl I have found to be so calm and polite. You know what I mean 😀 just a joke.

I was talking to both of them separately, and never even a single thought crossed my mind that they could like each other in the future. Back in the old days, Yaza used to walk around with a hockey stick, swinging it like a kung fu stick in an action movie. I never thought that one day, he would accidentally hit someone with it, and she would hit him right back. That is exactly what happened with him and Laura. For years, they were emotionally swinging and missing, ignoring the tension, ignoring each other's hits, and both just standing there acting completely casual like nothing was happening. Then, one day, BAM. They hit each other on their heads and fell down. Not a "need an ambulance" thing, but the kind of going down where you are "falling in love."

I saw this stage from a distance, watching them slowly build a rocket ship, strap each other in, and launch themselves to the moon. It still makes me wonder how they built something for years and hid it, especially when I cannot even hide a phone properly in my pocket.

If you are wondering why I am here to moderate their channel, it is not just because Yaza is my brother. It is because watching these two finally figure out love restored my faith in it. Trust, loyalty, and actually standing by someone feel like rare collectors' items in relationships these days. But seeing Yaza and Laura stick it out completely changed my perspective. Everything else in life can change, get added, or get taken away, like jobs, hobbies, or learning that gets forgotten, but love is the one thing that actually completes the picture. So, do not leave a loving partner, even if fighting the world is the only option. That is what their story taught me, and I hope I can make others understand the value of love too.
